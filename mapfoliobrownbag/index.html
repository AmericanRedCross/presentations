After the Haiti Earthquake in 2010 the need and desire for programs and disaster situational awareness drastically changed. the haiti earthquake was a huge catalyst to change how the American Red Cross opperated and managed our data.
We were living in the pre-cloud era, what data we did have was in different excel files scattered around the organization.
basically we all had our own silos, silos that needed to come down
which basically meant that even if we wanted to we couldn't easily talk to one antoher and share data about even the most basic things like where are projects were.
a long time ago someone at the red cross said we need a system. A way to organize our data. It would be magical.
However, the organization and the technology were not ready yet. This process was 17 years in the making.
Now that the organizations data was put into Salesforce we had one huge problem it wasn't spatial data. Salesforce lacks an out of the box solution to geospatial locations.
Its around this time that I joined the Red Cross. My first assignment was to show salesforce data on a map.
my brilliant idea was to let someone way smarter than me try to figure it out. I met Ryan at the first JS.geo a while back and thought, hey he was cool. "somehow" they managed to bet out a couple of closed source companies that will remain nameless. Being in DC we didn't want the typical DC beltway bandit, we wanted someone that had dealt with humanitarian issues before
so we hired SpatialDev and they got to work, we tried some stuff and mostly people left us alone becaue ECOS was and is so much more important to the GIS success.
before I show the site I want to caution you that the data you see in the system is current as of last night, we update our database every 24 hours.
Our number one improvement for this year for the site is to make it even faster.
Please bear in mind that we still have a little bit of work to do on the site but we believe its good enough to use right now.
To keep the data secure we require that you use your salesforce credentials.
