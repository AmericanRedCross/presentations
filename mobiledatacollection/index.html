There are many reasons to do mobile data collection. most of which are that it allows you to focus on results and analysis and less on managing paper based forms.

Benefits

Faster

Surveying and encoding combined into one step

Analysis can happen in near real time

Becaue you don't have to collect and then enter data the turn around time for data response is much much faster. M&E experts can identify trends and/or gaps during data collection.

Accuracy

Automatic skip logic and constraint checks

No encoding means no transcription errors

If survey data isn't acurate its utility is very minimal. Accuracy is extremly important and mobile data collection ensures digestable responses.

Informative

GPS coordinates, photos, automatic timestamps, etc.

Mobile data collection allows enumerators to gather photos and other data that traditionally not easily gathered including photos, gps coordinates, and accurate timestamps.

Uses

Data Analysis doesn't just have to happen using excel.

GeoSpatial Analysis

For Example, trends not easily identifiable are easily spoted using geospatial analysis

Information Products

Promoting improved information products (dashboard solutions, maps, interactive graphs, etc.)

planning & implementaion

Reporting

Analysing and reporting on past achievments is very hard using traditional methods. Most of the time you send away your completed survey and in one or two months you get back the results. This paradigm isn't good enough, this slows down program design and hinders programs from having early impacts.

Sharing

Open data formats, open source software, and open community

Many Humanitarian organizations tend to keep methods and data private. Using ODK and other open technologies allows us to potentially share data more easily with other organizations inside and outside the Red Cross movement

Final Notes

Good data is the goal, mobile data collection is the tool

It's not about the gear, it's about the data
