Haiyan made landfall on November 8th with sustained winds of 195mph with gusts of 235mph, a storm surge of 6 meters with waves as high as 12 meters, and affected around 16 million people.[1]
Where should we go? The global Red Cross network would deploy some 200+ international delegates.
Data Science and Information Management is really pretty boring most of the time. People don't realize how repetative it is.
Sometimes we have to "steal" data
Sometimes we get to much data and are overwhelmed as in the case with Ebola, to many people are producing crap datasets.
People get too excited about the posibilities sometimes, putting us in a position of failure no matter what.
The other side of that coin is people not wanting the products that we make. It is now easier for us to make maps and other products than it is for people to consume those products.
Just remember to do good no matter what that good is. Remember that others are doing good too, that collaboration is much better than silos.
Work smarter, lets build new tools that allow us to focus on making decisions and not on collecting or cleaning data.
