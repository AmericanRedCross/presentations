The first 72 hours after an event look a little like this
Working a response is a grind, long hours, lots of stress, and workflows get really repetitive very quickly
coordination in the modern era really almost always happens through skype, sms, and twitter
people are getting on planes and we need to figure out the best data to give them, often its maps so they can have good understanding of the area
Without good data our work ends up being really really hard.
when we did start to get data it was often in pdf. PDF does not equal open data. Data must be machine readable processable and above all else require little to no work to reuse
Folks in the USG including the DoS HIU and National Geospatial Intelligence Agency were awesome and always gave us great relevant data in spatial formats we could use with open data licenses. When we got data either from the USG or other reliable places we would immediately share it with the HOT team to prioritize tasks for not only the hardest hit areas but the areas where Humanitarian aid would be going first and then working out from there.
Because we couldn't get other data we needed we started mining websites and reverse engineering web services to get data. We used an army of volunteers who donated time over weekends and at night to free data locked away in pdfs to create real usable data, this data included storm surge, population datasets, and transportation information
Contributors made a few changes to the basemap
during disasters entire cities can be mapped in one day
1600+ contributors, 4.8 million edits, roughly 3 years of an active mapper or one bot
the coordination with HOT allowed us to fundamentally change the way we worked with our deployed staff
We started talking and embracing open data just over a year ago with a big commitment in the last 6 months. This event has proven and reinforced all of the reasons why open data and open platforms are awesome. At SOTM US last year just after joining Red Cross I asked the OSM and larger geospatial community to help people help people. You guys lived up to that request and then some.
Still more work to do. Build software, document stuff, and trace.
